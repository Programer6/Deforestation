Why Forests Matter


  • Biodiversity Loss: Many species live in trees. Loss of unique genetic codes, medicines and ecological services are also experienced as well.
  • Climate Crisis: Forests absorb carbon dioxide, serving as carbon sinks in nature. This means that when we clear-cut, we are releasing stored carbon thereby amplifying global warming.
  • Human Impact: By forests, the native communities mean nutrition, civilization, and a source of money. When deforestation takes place, this becomes disturbed.

What we can do?


  • Plant trees in your backyard or community.
  • Support organizations that work towards reforestation.
  • Reduce paper usage by going digital and using recycled paper products.
  • Choose sustainably sourced wood products and furniture.
  • Consume less meat or opt for sustainably sourced and certified products.
  • Recycle and reuse paper, cardboard, and other forest-based products.
  • Support companies that use sustainable and responsible forestry practices.
  • Advocate for stricter regulations and policies against deforestation.
  • Participate in community initiatives and tree-planting events.
  • Spread awareness about the importance of forests and the consequences of deforestation.

Here are some way you can help now:


  • Support the Arbor Day Foundation that works towards reforestation.
  • Donate to Rainforest Action Network to protect forests and indigenous rights.
